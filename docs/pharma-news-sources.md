## Pharmaceutical industry / life sciences / biotech

### Swissmedic
**Why it belongs:** For a Swiss audience, Swissmedic is the obvious pharma analogue to FINMA. It is highly relevant to regulatory expectations around AI in medicinal products, submissions, evidence, and lifecycle oversight.

**Use for:**
- Swiss regulatory position on AI in medicinal products
- authorisation expectations
- signals on digital transformation and regulator posture

---

### EMA — Artificial Intelligence in the medicines lifecycle
**Why it belongs:** EMA’s AI work covers guidance and policy for AI throughout the medicines lifecycle. This makes it highly relevant to model validation, lifecycle controls, and evidence expectations for AI used in pharma R&D, clinical development, manufacturing, and post-marketing contexts.

**Use for:**
- EU medicines-regulation view of AI
- lifecycle governance
- regulatory-scientific workplans touching AI evaluation

---

### FDA CDER — Artificial Intelligence for Drug Development
**Why it belongs:** FDA is one of the most important global sources for AI in pharma. It is especially useful for tracking how AI is being used in submissions across nonclinical, clinical, postmarketing, and manufacturing phases.

**Use for:**
- US regulatory expectations
- AI in drug-development submissions
- examples of where AI is already appearing in real regulatory workflows

---

### FDA guidance: AI for regulatory decision-making
**Why it belongs:** This is especially relevant to probabilistic testing, validation, credibility, and evidence. It is directly concerned with the use of AI to support regulatory decision-making on drug and biological products.

**Use for:**
- credibility assessment of AI models
- model validation logic
- evidence expected when AI outputs support regulatory claims

---

### FDA — Guiding Principles of Good AI Practice in Drug Development
**Why it belongs:** A strong official source on what “good practice” looks like for AI in pharma, especially in relation to reliability, governance, and patient safety.

**Use for:**
- governance principles
- reliability and quality framing
- internal policy baselines for pharma AI engineering

---

### ICH
**Why it belongs:** Even where AI-specific language is still emerging, ICH remains central to harmonised expectations in pharmaceuticals. It matters because AI systems in pharma must ultimately fit into existing global quality, safety, and efficacy frameworks.

**Use for:**
- quality, safety, efficacy and lifecycle expectations
- harmonised norms that AI systems in pharma must fit into
- bridge between AI methods and existing pharma quality systems

---

### DIA (Drug Information Association)
**Why it belongs:** DIA is one of the strongest cross-functional sources at the boundary between regulators, pharma, biotech, and vendors. It is often where emerging discussions around AI, validation, clinical data, safety, and evidence appear in practical form.

**Use for:**
- regulatory-science discussion
- conferences and working-group signals
- practical industry interpretation of evolving expectations

---

### ISPE
**Why it belongs:** ISPE is especially relevant if the interest extends beyond discovery into GxP operations, manufacturing, computerised systems, CSV/CSA, and data integrity. It is where “interesting model” turns into “validated operational system”.

**Use for:**
- GxP validation context
- manufacturing and quality systems
- operational assurance for AI-enabled processes

---

### PDA (Parenteral Drug Association)
**Why it belongs:** PDA is useful where AI intersects with pharmaceutical quality, manufacturing science, sterility, and quality risk management. It is not AI-first, but highly relevant for monitoring how quality disciplines absorb advanced analytics and AI.

**Use for:**
- pharmaceutical quality operations
- manufacturing assurance
- practical quality and compliance interpretation

---

### MHRA
**Why it belongs:** The UK is outside the EU but still highly relevant to European pharma practice. MHRA is worth monitoring for guidance and consultation activity touching software, AI, evidence, and lifecycle regulation in medicines and related regulated products.

**Use for:**
- UK regulatory developments
- comparative regulatory thinking
- cross-border pharma compliance signals

---

### Endpoints News
**Why it belongs:** One of the strongest specialist outlets for biotech and pharma business/science news. Particularly good for tracking where AI is actually being deployed in pipelines, partnerships, and translational strategy.

**Use for:**
- fast-moving biotech and pharma developments
- AI deal flow and platform-company moves
- executive and industry sentiment

**Caveat:** Much of the best material is paywalled.

---

### Fierce Biotech
**Why it belongs:** One of the most useful specialist sources for biotech news, including AI and machine-learning developments in drug discovery, platform companies, and clinical development.

**Use for:**
- biotech AI announcements
- discovery and development partnerships
- operationally relevant industry news

---

### Fierce Pharma
**Why it belongs:** Complements Fierce Biotech by covering the larger commercial-pharma side: strategy, launches, operations, manufacturing, and market-facing developments, including AI-related moves.

**Use for:**
- big pharma strategy
- enterprise AI adoption by major manufacturers
- market and organisational signals

---

### BioCentury
**Why it belongs:** A high-value source for strategic biopharma intelligence, with substantive coverage of AI in biopharma, including its impact on target discovery, preclinical work, clinical operations, and partnering.

**Use for:**
- higher-end strategic analysis
- investment, partnerships, and platform credibility
- nuanced assessment of AI’s practical impact

**Caveat:** Largely subscription-based.

---

### pharmaphorum
**Why it belongs:** Regular and explicit coverage of AI in drug discovery and development, including partnerships, tooling, and implementation trends inside pharma.

**Use for:**
- readable industry updates
- AI round-ups
- practical visibility into what pharma teams are actually trying

---

### Drug Target Review
**Why it belongs:** A specialised source focused on drug discovery and development science, with recurring coverage of AI in drug discovery and its operational prospects.

**Use for:**
- drug-discovery methods
- AI tooling and technique trends
- scientific and industrial commentary

---

### Nature Biotechnology
**Why it belongs:** Valuable when higher-signal scientific and industry analysis is needed rather than pure journalism. Useful for sober assessment of AI claims and translational impact.

**Use for:**
- scientific and translational context
- sober assessment of AI claims
- industry trend pieces with stronger editorial filtering

---

### Nature Reviews Drug Discovery
**Why it belongs:** Strong source for deep context on drug-discovery trends, therapeutic strategy, and emerging technology. Not a fast-news outlet, but ideal for separating durable signal from hype.

**Use for:**
- strategic background reading
- scientific context around new platforms
- medium-term understanding of how AI is changing R&D

---

### STAT
**Why it belongs:** Broader than pure pharma, but often very good on biotech, drug development, regulation, clinical evidence, and industry narratives. Helps connect boardroom claims about AI to medical, scientific, and policy reality.

**Use for:**
- broader biotech/pharma journalism
- clinical and regulatory debate
- independent reporting on industry claims
