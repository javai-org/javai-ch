# Article 13: Transparency and Provision of Information to Deployers

Source: https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-13

## Overview
Article 13 requires high-risk AI systems to operate with sufficient transparency so deployers can interpret outputs and use them appropriately.

## Key Requirements

**Design Transparency**: Systems must be "designed and developed in such a way as to ensure that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret a system's output and use it appropriately."

**Instructions for Use**: Providers must supply instructions in digital or other appropriate formats containing "concise, complete, correct and clear information that is relevant, accessible and comprehensible to deployers."

## Required Information in Instructions

The instructions must include:

- Provider identity and contact details
- System characteristics, capabilities, and performance limitations, including intended purpose and accuracy metrics
- Known or foreseeable risks to health, safety, or fundamental rights
- Technical capabilities for explaining outputs
- Performance specifications for specific persons or groups
- Input data and training dataset specifications
- Interpretation guidance for system outputs
- Pre-determined performance changes
- Human oversight measures and technical interpretation tools
- Computational requirements and expected system lifetime
- Maintenance, care measures, and update protocols
- Log collection and storage mechanisms
