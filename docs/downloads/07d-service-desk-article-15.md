# Article 15: Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity

Source: https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-15

## Overview
This article establishes mandatory requirements for high-risk AI systems to maintain appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity throughout their operational lifecycle.

## Key Requirements

### Accuracy and Performance Standards
High-risk AI systems must "achieve an appropriate level of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity, and that they perform consistently" throughout their existence. Accuracy metrics and levels must be documented in instructions for use.

### Robustness Measures
Systems should withstand errors and faults that may arise internally or environmentally. The regulation permits "technical redundancy solutions, which may include backup or fail-safe plans." For continuously learning systems, developers must eliminate or minimize risks from biased outputs creating harmful feedback loops.

### Cybersecurity Protections
High-risk AI systems require resilience "against attempts by unauthorised third parties to alter their use, outputs or performance." Technical solutions should address AI-specific vulnerabilities including:
- Data poisoning (manipulating training datasets)
- Model poisoning (corrupting pre-trained components)
- Adversarial examples (crafted inputs causing incorrect outputs)
- Confidentiality breaches
- Model flaws

## Implementation Support
The Commission will collaborate with stakeholders to develop benchmarks and measurement methodologies for evaluating appropriate accuracy and robustness levels.
