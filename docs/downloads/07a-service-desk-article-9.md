# Article 9: Risk Management System

Source: https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-9

## Overview
Article 9 mandates that providers establish, implement, document, and maintain risk management systems for high-risk AI systems throughout their entire lifecycle.

## Key Requirements

**Continuous Process**: The system operates as an "iterative process planned and run throughout the entire lifecycle" with regular review and updates.

**Four-Step Framework**:
1. Identify known and reasonably foreseeable risks to health, safety, or fundamental rights during intended use
2. Estimate and evaluate risks from intended use and "reasonably foreseeable misuse"
3. Evaluate additional risks from post-market monitoring data
4. Adopt appropriate risk management measures

**Risk Mitigation**: Measures should focus on risks that are "reasonably mitigated or eliminated through the development or design" or through technical information provision.

**Testing Requirements**: High-risk systems must undergo testing "at any time throughout the development process, and, in any event, prior to their being placed on the market or put into service." Testing uses predefined metrics and probabilistic thresholds appropriate to intended purpose.

**Special Considerations**: Providers must assess potential adverse impacts on minors and vulnerable groups when relevant to the system's purpose.

**Integration**: Existing risk management processes under other EU laws can incorporate these requirements.
