# General-Purpose AI Models in the AI Act — Q&A

Source: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/general-purpose-ai-models-ai-act-questions-answers

## Why do we need rules for general-purpose AI models?

General-purpose AI models form the foundation for many downstream systems. The AI Act ensures these models are "safe and trustworthy" by requiring providers to maintain technical documentation, establish copyright policies, and publish training content summaries. For models posing systemic risks, additional obligations include risk assessment, incident reporting, and cybersecurity measures.

## What are general-purpose AI models?

The AI Act defines them as models displaying "significant generality" capable of performing "a wide range of distinct tasks" across applications. Key indicators include models with at least one billion parameters trained with large-scale self-supervision.

## What are general-purpose AI models with systemic risk?

These represent "large-scale harm" risks from state-of-the-art models or equivalently impactful ones. The AI Act sets a 10^25 FLOP training threshold to identify such models, though the Commission may adjust this threshold as technology evolves.

## What is a provider of a general-purpose AI model?

A provider is "a natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body that develops a general-purpose AI model or that has such a model developed and places it on the market" for payment or free distribution.

## What are the obligations for providers?

Providers must:
- Document technical information for regulators and downstream users
- Establish copyright compliance policies
- Publish detailed training content summaries

Providers of systemic-risk models additionally must assess/mitigate risks, document incidents, and ensure cybersecurity.

## Open-source exception

Models released under free open-source licenses with publicly available parameters, architecture, and usage information are exempted from documentation obligations — except systemic-risk models, which retain full obligations.

## Fine-tuning and modifications

Downstream entities modifying existing models may become providers of new models. An indicative threshold: modifications using over one-third of the original model's training compute constitute a new general-purpose AI model requiring compliance documentation.

## The General-Purpose AI Code of Practice

This industry-developed framework details compliance methods for Articles 53 and 55 obligations. Adherence is mitigating in enforcement decisions.

## AI Office enforcement powers

The AI Office supervises obligations under Article 88, possessing authority to request information, conduct evaluations, demand risk mitigations, and impose fines up to 3% of global annual turnover or €15 million, whichever is greater.
